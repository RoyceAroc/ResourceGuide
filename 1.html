Laws of Thermodynamics

  • First Law of Thermodynamics
    • - Energy can neither be created nor destroyed; it can only be transferred and transformed.
    - Ex: In photosynthesis, radiant energy from the Sun is converted to chemical energy in the form of the bonds that hold together atoms in carbohydrates.
  • Second Law of Thermodynamics
    • - This law says that the entropy (disorder) of the universe is increasing.
    - In most energy transformations, a significant fraction of the energy is lost to the universe as heat.
    - Ex: In food chains only about 10% of the energy from one trophic level is available for the next energy level upon consumption.
